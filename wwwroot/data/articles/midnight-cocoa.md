The house is quiet. The tree lights are the only ones still on. Somewhere between putting out the last plate of cookies and checking the locks, you find yourself standing in the kitchen with a saucepan in hand, heating milk for one more cup before the night ends.

## The Real Recipe

Forget the packets. Real midnight cocoa starts with whole milk warmed slowly — never boiled — with a heavy tablespoon of Dutch-process cocoa whisked in. Add a pinch of sea salt, a splash of vanilla, and just enough sugar to let the chocolate speak without shouting.

The marshmallows matter more than you think. The homemade kind, the ones that are pillowy and slightly sticky and taste like vanilla clouds, transform the cup from a drink into a ritual. You can make them in advance — cornstarch-dusted and cut into imperfect cubes that melt into the surface like small icebergs in a dark sea.

## The Hour Matters

There's something about cocoa after eleven that changes its character entirely. During the day it's a treat. At midnight it's a companion. The warmth in your hands, the steam curling toward a cold window, the way the first sip always burns just slightly — it's a small act of presence in a season that moves too fast.

> Midnight cocoa isn't about the cocoa. It's about the midnight.

Find a window. Don't check your phone. Let the mug get cool enough to hold with both hands and just sit there, watching whatever the night decides to show you — frost on glass, a streetlight through bare branches, maybe even snow.
