The world has gone quiet. Streetlamps still hum, but the traffic has not yet remembered how to roar. Snow, or perhaps only a fine frost, settles on your eyelashes and melts into the smallest of prayers as you step onto the path.

## The First Breath

You pull your coat tighter, and the wool hugs your shoulders like a familiar hand. The air is sharp — clean enough to feel it cut through the morning fog and settle on your tongue as a memory of mint and iron. Each inhale is a small vow to begin again.

> The cold does not erase wonder; it polishes it until it gleams.

## Footsteps and Forest Lights

On the frozen gravel, your boots make a gentle complaint, a soft crunch that keeps rhythm with your heartbeat. Somewhere, a dog barks once, then thinks better of it. You pass the dark windows of houses where curlers glow and tea simmers, and you carry a little of that unspoken warmth with you.

## The Slow Art of Arrival

You do not need a destination. The turning of your own breath is enough compass. Trees stand like patient sentinels, their bare branches etched against a sky the color of cooled ash. In this hush, thoughts loosen their grip and drift, one by one, like snow settling on a fence.

When you finally turn back toward the house, the doorknob feels heavier, as if you have carried the whole morning inside your chest. Steam rises from the kettle, and the room smells of cinnamon and wool — proof that the world, though quiet, is still paying attention.

'The world is full of beautiful things, and very few people have the leisure to see them.'

You have taken the time. That is the quiet magic.
