## Why Start a Holiday Journal?

Picture this: the scent of cinnamon and evergreen clinging to the air, a steaming mug of spiced chai in your hands, and the quiet hum of snowfall against the window. Starting a holiday journal isn’t about perfection—it’s about *presence*. The ritual of writing by hand slows time down, letting you savor moments that might otherwise blur into the cozy haze of celebrations. What do you carry into December? A grandmother’s recipe for gingerbread? The way your child’s laughter echoes through an empty hallway? These fragments, when written down, become heirlooms.

> *‘A journal is a place for secrets, not lies,’* says author A.H. Benjamin. *But in December, it’s also a place for unspoken joy—the warmth of a shared meal, the pause before lighting the last candle.*


## Finding Your Moment

Your journal shouldn’t compete with the chaos of the season. Maybe it’s before the morning rush, by the fire while the world sleeps, or during a rare quiet hour between guests. Even five minutes with a pen can anchor you. Choose a prompt that feels inviting: *What peace did you feel today?* or *What scent do you associate with winter magic?* There’s no wrong way to respond. Let your hand guide you—spirals, fragments, raw thoughts. The texture of the paper, the smell of ink, the chill or warmth of your surroundings—these details make the practice visceral.

## What to Write: More Than Just Memories

Don’t limit yourself to recounting events. Journaling is a dialogue. Ask: *What am I hoping for this holiday season?* *What fears need to sit with me tonight?* *What small act of joy did I witness?* This practice is a bridge between the external busyness of the season and the internal stillness we all crave. Let your entries evolve—some days might be poems, others just a single word like ‘gratitude’ scribbled over a smudge of coffee stain. Over time, you’ll build a tapestry of your holiday experience, thread by thread.
