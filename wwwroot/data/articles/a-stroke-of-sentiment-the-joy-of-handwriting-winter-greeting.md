## Why Handwritten Cards Still Matter

In a world where emails vanish into digital ether and eCards flicker like distant pixels, there’s a quiet rebellion in putting pen to paper. Kids might giggle at the absurdity of a grown adult signing their name in cursive, but elders recognize the truth: handwritten cards carve a path through the noise. They land differently—a crumpled envelope on a kitchen table feels like an invitation, not an assignment. The imperfect lines, the occasional smudge, make them *alive*.

There’s also the responsibility of craft. Choosing the right paper (thick enough to hold ink but softly yielding to your touch) or selecting stationery that mirrors the recipient’s spirit—these details matter. Maybe you opt for a recycled deck with a maple-scented coating, or perhaps you source a vintage envelope from a thrift store, its wax seal still intact. Every choice whispers, *This was made with intention.*
