## The Quiet Joy of Ink and Paper

You might think I’m nostalgic for a dying practice, but let me ask you this: Does the gentle scratch of a quill against handmade paper not feel *alive*? I remember the thrill of addressing an envelope with care, folding it like a secret, and slipping it into a mailbox just in time for Christmas. The postman’s silence about it, the thrill of waiting for a reply—these were rituals, not just gestures.

There’s a reason letters are disappearing. Digital messages are faster, but they lack the clumsy sincerity of a paper cut or the floral scent of sealing wax. I once found a stack of my grandmother’s letters under her attic rug. Each bore her careful handwriting, notes about a neighbor’s new goat, a recipe for spiced gingerbread, and a dash of wit: *’Your skiing skills are improving, I see’*—she’d circled that phrase in red.
